Vibroacoustic Therapy

Touchless meditative massage

What it is

Vibroacoustic Therapy (VAT) combines low-frequency sound vibrations with therapeutic music. You recline on a vibroacoustic bed with embedded tactile transducers while using headphones — creating a calming, whole-body experience.

Benefits & support areas

  • Stress & anxiety support
  • Pain & discomfort support
  • Muscle tension & spasticity support
  • Sleep disturbances support
  • Mood & emotional well-being
  • Mental clarity & focus

What to expect

  • Arrive in comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Recline on the vibroacoustic bed (headphones + optional eye mask)
  • Frequencies selected based on your needs
  • Relax and let sound + vibration do the work
  • Sessions last 15–60 minutes, based on your goals

Our Mission

Our intention is to offer simple, mindful tools that create meaningful shifts in how we move, breathe, and live through gentle, accessible yoga and heart-centered guidance.

We support others in reconnecting with themselves — finding calm in the everyday, strength in softness, and balance both on and off the mat. We meet you where you are, honoring your body, your breath, and your journey. Every breath, every pose, and every moment of awareness is an invitation to return home to peace.

Meet Tara

Tara Copp is the founder of Tarasana Yoga and a certified yoga instructor specializing in gentle, restorative, and accessible practices.

After spending over thirty years in the service industry, Tara knows firsthand how hard constant movement and long hours can be on the body and mind. Her own journey through pain and fatigue led her to yoga — a practice that taught her how to slow down, listen inward, and rebuild balance from the inside out.

Now, through her teachings with Tarasana at CareMore Health & Wellness, she shares what she’s learned: caring for your body is not a luxury, but an essential act of growth. Her classes offer a calm space to breathe, soften, and reconnect — one mindful moment at a time.

“I believe in the quiet power of simple practices — a slow breath, a gentle stretch, a steady vibration. These are the moments that help us return home to peace.” — Tara Copp
