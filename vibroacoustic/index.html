Vibroacoustic Therapy

Touchless meditative massage

Vibroacoustic Therapy (VAT) is a holistic wellness approach that combines low-frequency sound vibrations with specially composed therapeutic music. You recline on a vibroacoustic bed equipped with embedded tactile transducers while using headphones, creating a calming, whole-body experience.

What it is

VAT uses gentle vibrations (often in the 20–120 Hz range) transmitted through the body’s water-rich tissues to encourage relaxation, release tension, and restore balance.

Benefits & support areas

  • Stress & anxiety support
  • Pain & discomfort support
  • Muscle tension & spasticity support
  • Sleep disturbances support
  • Mood & emotional well-being
  • Mental clarity & focus

What to expect

  • Arrive in comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Recline on the vibroacoustic bed (headphones + optional eye mask)
  • Frequencies selected based on your needs
  • Relax and let sound + vibration do the work
  • Sessions last 15–60 minutes, based on your goals

Session length options

15 minutes

Quick stress relief, power naps, and an introduction for first-time users.

30 minutes

A full therapeutic experience for relaxation and wellness maintenance.

45 minutes

Deeper meditation states and more comprehensive stress reduction.

60 minutes

Deepest experience with multiple frequencies for chronic concerns and profound relaxation.

Therapeutic frequencies (overview)

30–40 Hz (Gamma range)

Associated with cognitive function, attention, memory, and mental clarity. Often used for supporting focus and neurological wellness.

40–60 Hz

Often linked with energizing effects, improved alertness, and enhanced mood.

60–80 Hz

Connected to balance, harmony, and deep relaxation — supporting physical and emotional equilibrium.

80–120 Hz

Associated with gentle stimulation, circulation support, and muscle relaxation — often used as a complement to recovery.

Note: This is wellness support information, not medical advice. If you have health concerns or implanted devices, please consult your medical provider.
